Robot Design

Subsystems

SYS-01

Drive

The drivetrain is the robot's mobility system. Our robot utilizes a swerve drive system, offering exceptional agility and precision by allowing independent control of each wheel's speed and direction — enabling fast, dynamic movement in all directions.

SYS-02

Claw & Wrist

The claw grabs and holds game pieces using motor-driven wheels. The wrist mechanism allows the claw to tilt to specific angles, optimizing intake and scoring positions — providing versatility across varying game piece sizes.

SYS-03

Photon Vision

Photon Vision leverages cameras and image processing to determine target distance, angle, and position. The system primarily tracks AprilTags on the field, enabling precise autonomous alignment and scoring.

SYS-04

Elevator

The elevator enables the robot to reach different heights for intaking and scoring. It uses two motors with multiple setpoints corresponding to field heights — providing smooth, controlled vertical movement across all game levels.

SYS-05

Climber

The climber is a critical endgame mechanism. Our robot is equipped for a deep climb, ascending to higher levels by engaging the cage. Precise control and timing helps secure valuable ranking points in the final moments of every match.
